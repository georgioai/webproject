Internet Infrastructure

The Internet infrastructure comprises the physical and logical systems that facilitate global connectivity. Key components include:

  • Physical Transmission Media: Fiber optic cables, satellites, and wireless networks
  • Routing and Forwarding: Routers and switches that direct data traffic
  • Naming and Numbering Systems: Domain Name System (DNS) and IP addressing
  • Security and Identity Protection: Protocols ensuring secure data exchange

These elements work in unison to maintain the robustness and reliability of the Internet.

History of the Web

The World Wide Web was invented by Sir Tim Berners-Lee in 1989 at CERN. Initially designed to meet the demand for information sharing among physicists, it introduced:

  • HTML: HyperText Markup Language for structuring web content
  • HTTP: HyperText Transfer Protocol for communication
  • Web Browsers: Software to access and navigate the Web

The Web has since evolved into a ubiquitous platform for information, commerce, and social interaction.

Internet Statistics – Middle East and Lebanon

The Middle East has experienced significant growth in Internet usage. For instance:

  • Iran: Approximately 46.8 million users
  • Saudi Arabia: Approximately 18.3 million users
  • Lebanon: Approximately 5.7 million users

Lebanon has shown remarkable progress, with increasing broadband penetration and mobile Internet adoption. However, challenges remain in infrastructure development and service affordability.
